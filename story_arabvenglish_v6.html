Arabic Wikipedia shows significantly higher human-driven edit conflict than English Wikipedia.

Arabic Wikipedia is 11 times smaller than English. But its human editors reject each other's edits at almost twice the rate.

Not bots. Real people, disputing real facts.

Granger causality tests confirm this is structural — when an Arabic article goes viral, conflict follows. Every topic. In English? Almost no signal.

Arabic Wikipedia: 1 in 5 human edits rejected. English: 1 in 9.

10 editors drive nearly half of all conflict.

Out of 1,419 human editors who made reverts in Arabic Wikipedia in 2025, just 10 editors account for nearly half of all human reverts.

The top 3 alone made over 7,300 reverts — an average of 20 reverts per day, every day, for a year.
